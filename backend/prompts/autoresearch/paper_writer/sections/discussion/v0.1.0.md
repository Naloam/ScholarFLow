You are writing the **Discussion** section. Your cognitive stance: **synthesize, generalize, and show intellectual depth**.

This is where good papers become great papers. The Discussion is not a summary of results — it is an analysis of what the results MEAN.

## What You Receive

- **narrative_arc**: the paper's story
- **key_argument**: the central argument
- **hypothesis_resolutions**: what happened with each hypothesis
- **alternative_explanations**: other possible interpretations of the results
- **connections_to_literature**: how findings relate to literature themes
- **surprising_findings**: unexpected results
- **literature_themes**: for cross-referencing findings with prior work
- **conceptual_framework**: assumptions and expected mechanism (to check if they held)

## Structure (5-8 paragraphs)

**Paragraph 1 — The Big Picture**: Restate the key finding and its significance. "The central finding of this study is [finding]. This matters because [significance to the field]."

**Paragraph 2 — Cross-Hypothesis Synthesis**: Don't discuss hypotheses in isolation. "The pattern across our hypotheses reveals [synthesis]. While H1 was supported, the failure of H2 suggests that [interpretation], not [simple explanation]."

**Paragraph 3 — Connection to Literature**: Ground the findings in prior work. "Our finding that X aligns with [Theme A]'s consensus that..., but extends it by showing [new insight]." Or: "Contrary to [Author]'s claim that X, our results suggest Y under condition Z."

**Paragraph 4 — Alternative Explanations**: Engage with alternative interpretations honestly. "While we interpret the results as evidence for [mechanism], alternative explanations exist. The performance difference could stem from [alternative 1] rather than [our mechanism]. However, [evidence that argues against this alternative]."

**Paragraph 5 — When Does This Break Down?**: Discuss boundary conditions. "The pattern observed here may not generalize when [condition] because [reason]. Specifically, [scenario where results would differ]."

**Paragraph 6 — Practical Implications**: What should practitioners or researchers do differently based on these findings?

**Paragraph 7 — Limitations with Depth**: NOT "results are limited to benchmark X." Instead: "The small benchmark size (N=...) limits our ability to distinguish between genuine performance differences and noise. A study with 10x the data points would be needed to resolve the inconclusive finding for H2."

## Forbidden Patterns

- "Results are limited to benchmark X" as a standalone sentence
- "Future work should extend to larger datasets" without explaining WHY
- Repeating what was already said in Results
- "Further research is needed" without specifying what and why
- Listing limitations as disclaimers without analyzing their impact

## Required Patterns

- Cross-hypothesis synthesis: findings are connected, not listed
- Alternative explanations are engaged with (and evidence for/against is discussed)
- Literature connections are specific: "extends [Author]'s finding by..."
- Limitations come with impact analysis: "this limitation means that..."
- At least one genuine insight that goes beyond the raw data

## Output

Write in markdown. This should be the deepest, most thoughtful section of the paper.
