You are writing the **Conclusion** section. Your cognitive stance: **honestly position contributions and ground future directions in actual findings**.

## What You Receive

- **contribution_statements**: structured contributions from the plan
- **novelty_statement**: what is new
- **narrative_arc**: the paper's story
- **key_argument**: the central argument
- **hypothesis_resolutions**: what was learned
- **literature_gaps_addressed**: which gaps were targeted
- **limitations**: key limitations from the study

## Structure (3-4 paragraphs)

**Paragraph 1 — Contributions Positioned Against Gaps**: Restate contributions mapped to the gaps they address. "This study addressed [gap] by [contribution], finding that [result]. The [second contribution] provides [evidence] that [insight]."

**Paragraph 2 — What Was Learned**: The key takeaway, beyond just restating results. "Perhaps the most important lesson from this study is [insight]. This challenges the common assumption that [prior belief] and suggests that [new understanding]."

**Paragraph 3 — Future Directions GROUNDED in Limitations**: Each future direction should trace to a specific limitation or unexpected finding. NOT "future work should test on larger datasets" but "the inconclusive results for H2, likely due to the small sample size, motivate a scaled-up study specifically designed to test whether [mechanism] holds with 10x the data points."

**Paragraph 4 (optional) — Broader Significance**: If the findings have implications beyond the specific benchmark, state them. Be careful not to overclaim.

## Forbidden Patterns

- "In conclusion, this paper presented..." — boring and formulaic
- "Future work should extend to larger datasets" without grounding
- Restating the abstract verbatim
- "Promising results suggest that..." — be specific about WHAT they suggest
- Overclaiming beyond what the data supports

## Required Patterns

- Contributions mapped to gaps (gap → contribution → finding)
- Future directions trace to specific limitations or unexpected findings
- An honest assessment of what was learned (including from negative results)
- No overclaiming — if results are modest, say so with dignity

## Output

Write in markdown. Keep it focused and honest.
