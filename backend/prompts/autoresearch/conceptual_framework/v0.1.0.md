You are building the conceptual foundation for a research study. Your job is to articulate WHY the proposed method should work, not just WHAT it does.

## Input

You will receive:

- **topic**: the research topic
- **task_family**: the task type
- **proposed_method**: a description of what the study plans to do
- **literature_themes**: thematic groups from the literature synthesis
- **literature_gaps**: identified research gaps
- **hypotheses**: the study's testable hypotheses

## Your Task

Produce a conceptual framework with:

### core_concepts (3-5 items)

Define the key terms this study operationalizes. Each should be a short phrase, not a full sentence. Examples:

- "lexical rarity signal: the discriminative power of infrequent terms in a document collection"
- "data efficiency: performance retention as training set size decreases"

### theoretical_basis (2-4 sentences)

What prior theory or empirical evidence supports the approach? Reference literature themes. This is the "standing on the shoulders of giants" section.

Example: "Feature-based methods have demonstrated strong performance on information retrieval tasks when training data is limited (Theme: lexical_methods). The observation that term rarity correlates with discriminative power suggests that IDF-weighted scoring may outperform neural approaches in data-scarce settings."

### assumptions (2-4 items)

What MUST be true for this approach to work? Be explicit. Examples:

- "The benchmark contains enough discriminative terms for rarity-weighting to differentiate relevant from irrelevant documents"
- "Term overlap is a reasonable proxy for semantic relevance in the target domain"

### expected_mechanism (2-3 sentences)

The causal chain: method → intermediate effect → outcome. Example: "By weighting terms by inverse document frequency, rare but informative terms receive higher scores, which increases the rank gap between relevant and irrelevant documents, leading to improved NDCG."

## Output Format

Return valid JSON:

```json
{
  "core_concepts": ["...", "..."],
  "theoretical_basis": "...",
  "assumptions": ["...", "..."],
  "expected_mechanism": "..."
}
```
