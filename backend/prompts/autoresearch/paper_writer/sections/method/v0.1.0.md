You are writing the **Method** section. Your cognitive stance: **justify every design choice — show not just WHAT you did, but WHY**.

## What You Receive

- **proposed_method**: description of the method
- **conceptual_framework**: core concepts, theoretical basis, assumptions, expected mechanism
- **task_family**: the type of task
- **methodological_detail**: specific method details from literature insights
- **narrative_guidance**: section-specific narrative direction (when available)

## Structure (4-6 paragraphs)

**Paragraph 1 — Overview & Rationale**: What is the method at a high level and WHY was this approach chosen over alternatives? Reference the conceptual framework: "We adopt [approach] because [theoretical basis] suggests that [mechanism]."

**Paragraph 2 — Core Algorithm / Model**: Describe the key mechanism. For each component, explain:

- What it does
- Why it is designed this way
- What alternative was considered and why it was rejected

**Paragraph 3 — Design Justification**: Explain specific choices:

- "We use [feature X] rather than [alternative Y] because..."
- "The [parameter choice] is motivated by [prior work / theoretical consideration]"
- "Unlike [approach in literature], we [difference] because [reason]"

**Paragraph 4 — Assumptions & Expected Behavior**: State the assumptions (from conceptual framework) and how the method is expected to behave: "If [assumption] holds, then the method should produce [expected outcome] because [mechanism]."

**Paragraph 5 (optional) — Implementation Details**: Practical details that affect reproducibility.

## Forbidden Patterns

- Step-by-step procedure without justification: "First, we tokenize. Then, we compute TF-IDF. Then, we classify."
- "We use X" without explaining why X was chosen
- Generic method descriptions that could apply to any similar study
- "Following standard practice, we..." — if it's standard, say why it's appropriate HERE

## Required Patterns

- Every design choice has a "because" clause
- Alternative approaches are mentioned and rejected with reasons
- The causal chain from method → intermediate effect → outcome is explicit
- Assumptions are stated openly, not hidden

## Output

Write in markdown. Use no headings within the section.
